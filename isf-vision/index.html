01 / North Star

Why We're Rebuilding ISF

The North Star

ISF is the default tool for outcome-based selling — where every AE and SE goes to map a customer problem to a proven, deployable solution. Internally through Snowhouse and CoCo. Externally to partners and the Snowflake Developer Solutions site.

Today — What's Broken

  • Solutions reinvented across teams and regions — tribal knowledge
  • Inconsistent demo and messaging quality per engagement
  • Hard to connect a business problem to a proven execution path
  • Backend lives in Snowhouse — blocks partner and external access
  • No deployable assets attached to most solutions
  • No feedback loop — system doesn't improve over time
  • Solution quality is unpredictable — no formal gating today

Tomorrow — What ISF Becomes

  • Single source of truth: business problem → solution → deployable asset
  • Every solution = metadata + a working demo and/or a deploy path
  • Curated, high-quality catalog — quality enforced by Industry Councils
  • Data layer redesigned to enable partner and external access
  • Accessible via UI, CoCo skill/agent, and Snowflake Intelligence
  • Feedback-driven: usage signals improve recommendations over time
  • Solutions available to partners and the Snowflake Developer Solutions site

05 / Contribution Experience

Making It Easy to Contribute

We've heard the feedback. Contribution is too hard today — and if contributing is hard, the catalog stays stale.

The Problem Today

SEs have great work — POCs, demos, customer-specific solutions — but contributing it to ISF takes too long and requires too much manual effort. The form is overwhelming, the structure is unclear, and there's no guided path. So people don't contribute. And the ones who do often submit incomplete solutions. This is the core content quality problem.

Three Contribution Paths (in priority order)
Path 1 — Lowest Friction

Auto-Draft from Existing Work

  • Point ISF Creator at a POC, demo, or existing deck
  • System auto-generates a structured solution draft
  • Contributor reviews, fills gaps, and submits
  • Goal: contribution takes minutes, not hours
  • Reduces blank-page problem — most fields pre-filled
Path 2 — CoCo Guided

ISF Creator Skill

  • Conversational authoring via CoCo
  • Step-by-step prompts: problem → persona → use case → outcome → assets
  • Validates completeness as you go — no surprises at submit
  • Can attach GitHub links, demo URLs, architecture diagrams inline
  • Best for contributors who prefer a guided, interactive flow
Path 3 — UI Form

Redesigned Contribution UI

  • Streamlined form — only required fields shown upfront
  • Progressive disclosure: advanced fields unlocked after basics
  • Inline validation and completeness score as you fill
  • Duplicate detection: flags similar existing solutions before submit
  • Save draft at any point, resume later

Design Principles

  • Lightweight enough for SEs — contribution should feel like part of the workflow, not extra work
  • Structured enough for quality — the system enforces completeness so councils don't have to chase it
  • Automated wherever possible — reduce manual fields through inference and auto-draft
  • Duplicate-aware — prevent redundant solutions before they're created

What Makes Contribution Hard Today

  • Overwhelming number of fields — no clear sense of what's required vs. optional
  • No guided path — contributors start from blank, no templates or prompts
  • No auto-draft — all content must be manually typed or pasted
  • No inline validation — errors only surface at submit time
  • No feedback on completeness until after the fact

09 / Open Questions

Decisions Needed to Move Forward

Flagged for leadership alignment. Each blocks a specific downstream workstream.

Overarching

Do we agree on the phases and milestones?

  • Is the Phase 1 → Phase 2 → Phase 3 sequencing correct?
  • Are the right things in the right phases, or should anything move?
  • What is the engineering team's capacity and realistic timeline for Phase 1?
Partner Access

What can partners see, exactly?

  • Which specific fields are always internal-only regardless of visibility state?
  • Is the partner view the same as internal field minus confidential fields?
  • Who on the partner team is defining requirements — and when do we have clarity?
Backend / Data Layer

How do we make data externally accessible?

  • Data share from Snowhouse, or full migration to a new data layer?
  • What is the engineering estimate for this work?
  • How do we maintain continuity for the existing app during the transition?
Governance

Council authority and enforcement

  • Does leadership agree ISF is the standard and council approval is required to publish?
  • What happens if a council is slow to review — who escalates and on what timeline?
  • How do we handle legacy solutions that predate councils?
Repo / Plugin Standard

DevRel agreement and publishing ownership

  • Is CoCo plugin the official standard, or are there exceptions?
  • What is the formal agreement with DevRel on repo structure and CI/CD?
  • Who owns Snowflake Labs publishing — DevRel or the Industry Council?
Demo Hub / Compute

Who pays for externally-hosted demos?

  • Are we willing to pay Snowflake compute to serve demos externally to customers?
  • What's the cost model — per demo, shared pool, or sponsor account?
  • Which account hosts the demo hub and who owns it operationally?
