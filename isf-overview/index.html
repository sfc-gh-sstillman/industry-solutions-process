01 / What is ISF
The Core Engine — and How People Access It
ISF connects business problems → personas → use cases → validated solutions → actionable assets
The Problem Today
  • Field reinvents solutions across teams and regions — tribal knowledge, ad-hoc content
  • Inconsistent demo and messaging quality across engagements
  • Hard to connect a customer's business problem to a proven execution path
  • Existing ISF is overwhelming — too much content, unclear what to focus on, leaving users confused about where to start
  • Missing assets (especially customer-shareable demos) and poor discoverability
What ISF Becomes
  • A solution intelligence layer — the single source of truth for how Snowflake solves customer problems
  • Every solution = core metadata + an actionable solution package (skills plugin or deployable demo)
  • Curated, high-quality catalog — only validated solutions, automated freshness enforcement
  • Feedback-driven: usage signals improve recommendations over time
  • Accessible internally and externally through multiple surfaces
Where the field and customers access ISF
Internal / Field
ISF UI — browse, search, evaluate
Industry profiles in Snowhouse (e.g., HCLS: 26+ skills, zero setup)
Agents in Snowflake CoWork (e.g., MFG Concierge)
External / Partners & Customers
Developer solutions page
Partner-specific agent or skill for self-service browsing
Potential future: customer-facing solution exploration (not yet defined)
02 / What We're Building in the Product
Focus Areas for Rebuilding the ISF Core
Making ISF a tool the field can trust — high quality, easy to navigate, and always actionable
1
Clean Up & Redefine Solution Quality
Audit all existing solutions and remove anything that doesn't meet the quality bar. Redefine what fields a solution actually needs — slimming down the overwhelming content model so users aren't confused about what to focus on.
2
Simplify the UI
Make it fast and intuitive for the field to find what they need. Reduce friction in navigation, surface the most relevant solutions upfront, and cut the noise so AEs and SEs can get in and out quickly.
3
Improve Recommendations (WLC Integration)
Improve and simplify the recommendations experience in partnership with the WLC team. Account teams should clearly see what their customer's peers are doing and where the whitespace opportunities are — contextual and account-specific, not a generic list.
4
Regular Reviews & Freshness Enforcement
Set up automated scans to detect outdated content and flag anything that needs updating. The field needs to trust that what's in ISF is current — stale solutions are clearly marked or removed before they cause damage.
5
Every Solution = Metadata + Actionable Package
No solution ships without a deployable asset attached — either a skills plugin or a pre-built demo with a validated talk track. We're also creating clear guidance for consumers on how to deploy and use these packages so there's no ambiguity about how to get value from a solution.
⚙️
ISF Kit — CoCo Skills for Managing ISF
We're building CoCo skills to power these processes — with clear guidance and tooling for the field and industry specialists to contribute: ISF Reader (query & explore solutions), ISF Creator (author new solutions from POCs and demos), and ISF Contributor (update and maintain existing solutions). The goal is to make contributing to ISF as low-friction as possible.
03 / How Solutions Come to Life
Crowdsourced, Governed, Actionable
The Industry Skills Initiative is the mechanism for packaging and maintaining high-quality solution assets
Solution Packaging Model Defining
  • Every solution must include an actionable package: either a skills plugin (contextualizable, interactive) or a deployable pre-built demo with validated talk track
  • Working with DevRel on best practices for plugin packaging and publishing
  • CoCo plugins to help deploy solutions — clear standards for how assets are built and referenced
  • Platform vs. product skills will have defined rules for how they're referenced
Industry Councils HCLS Pilot
  • Cross-functional councils own quality, review, and maintenance of solutions per vertical
  • HCLS pilot in progress: Sales Leadership, PS, Industry Principals as governing body
  • 2-month pilot timeline → incorporate learnings → roll out to all other industries
  • Dedicated contributor launchpads for SEs to submit and refine skills
  • Slack channels per industry for field engagement (#coco-hcls-skills, etc.)

04 / The Plan
Three Parallel Tracks, One Coherent Direction
We're marching on the core platform, solution packaging, and governance simultaneously
🏗️
Track 1: Rebuild the Core
Platform & Content Quality
  • Simplify the solution data model — remove bloat, define minimum viable content
  • Audit all existing solutions; remove those that don't meet quality bar
  • Every solution must have a solution package — no packageless solutions in the catalog
  • Enforce automated freshness & completeness checks (auto-unpublish stale content)
  • Update ISF UI for faster discovery, trust signals, and asset visibility
⚙️
Track 2: Define the Packaging Standard
Skills & Solution Assets
  • Finalize plugin packaging format with DevRel — best practices, tooling, CI/CD
  • Build CoCo plugins to streamline solution deployment
  • Define rules for platform vs. product skill referencing
  • Establish contributor launchpad: SE submission → IA review → council approval
  • Crowd-source broadly, but be opinionated on quality gates
🏛️
Track 3: Scale Governance
Industry Councils & Rollout
  • HCLS council now active — proving out the model (2-month pilot)
  • Capture learnings on council structure, velocity, and quality enforcement
  • Roll out council model to remaining verticals post-pilot
  • Tie council ownership to solution lifecycle (publish, maintain, retire)
  • Establish success metrics: % solutions meeting bar, field adoption rate
The North Star
ISF becomes the default tool for outcome-based selling — where every AE and SE goes to map a customer problem to a proven, actionable solution. Internally through Snowhouse and CoWork agents. Externally through developer solutions and partner channels. Quality enforced by industry councils.
